# NP231 Transfer Case

## What the NP231 Is

The NP231 (Command-Trac) is the part-time 4WD transfer case used in all 4WD Jeep XJ
Cherokees. It has three positions:
- **2H** — 2-wheel drive high range (normal driving)
- **4H** — 4-wheel drive high range (loose surfaces, light off-road)
- **4L** — 4-wheel drive low range (technical off-road, maximum torque)

## Why the Selector Gate Matters for Firmware

The XJ's transmission selector gate is **P → R → N → D → 3 → 1**.

Engaging 4WD Low requires:
1. Vehicle fully stopped (or moving at less than 3 mph)
2. Transmission selector moved to **Neutral**
3. Transfer case lever moved to **4L**
4. Transmission selector returned to **Drive** (or 3 or 1)

Because the driver **must move through Neutral** to engage 4WD Low during a drive,
`currentGear` must be preserved when the selector passes through Neutral.

If `currentGear` were reset to 1 on every Neutral entry, the transmission would
always drop to 1st gear after a 4WD Low engagement — forcing an unintended downshift
at the moment the driver is trying to maintain momentum in a technical situation.

## Firmware Consequence

`justEnteredParkNeutral()` calls `solenoids.allOff()` but does NOT reset `currentGear`.
When the selector returns to Drive, `justEnteredDrive()` calls `solenoids.applyGear(currentGear)`
— re-engaging at the gear that was active before Neutral.

See `.agents/DECISIONS.md` ADR-001 for the full decision record.

**Do NOT add `currentGear = 1` to the `justEnteredParkNeutral()` handler.**

## 4WD Low and Gear Selection

When in 4WD Low, the effective gear ratios are already multiplied by the transfer case
reduction (approximately 2.72:1 for the NP231). This means:
- 1st gear in 4L is extremely low — appropriate for rock crawling
- Higher gears in 4L are useful for moderate-speed 4WD Low situations

The paddle shifter functions normally in 4WD Low — the driver can still shift between
gears 1–5 while in 4WD Low range.
